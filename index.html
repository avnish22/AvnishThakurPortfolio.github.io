Telecom Customer Churn Prediction

I worked on a telecom customer churn project where the goal was to identify why customers leave and predict who is at risk. I analyzed 7K+ customer records, performed statistical and feature analysis, and found that short-tenure, month-to-month contracts, and higher monthly charges were key churn drivers. I then built a logistic regression model with ~79% accuracy to predict churn, and delivered recommendations like incentivizing long-term contracts and focusing retention on early-tenure customers. This project shows both my ability to do deep analysis and apply machine learning to solve a real business problem.

Bank Marketing Campaign Response Prediction

I worked on a bank marketing campaign project where the goal was to improve campaign efficiency. I analyzed ~40K records of customer and campaign data, identified that middle-aged customers with higher balances and previous successful contacts were most likely to subscribe, while too many calls reduced success. I built a logistic regression model with ~68% accuracy as a baseline, and recommended improvements like resampling, advanced models, and campaign optimization strategies. This project demonstrates my ability to combine statistical analysis and machine learning to deliver business impact.
